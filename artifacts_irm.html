UNIT 3: Status Report Assignment


Assignment Brief:
Acme Manufacturing is an SME (Small/Medium Enterprise) employing 150 staff. It produces sporks for niche industries and due to increased demand, has decided to purchase an ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) system to allow itself to more flexibly manage its supply chain. With this, the company should be able to scale production up or down to better meet demand.

The company has shortlisted its selection down to 3 options:

  1. A COTS (Commercial Off the Shelf) solution provided by a major manufacturer at a purchase cost of $100k. Support costs vary depending on the level required but a basic telephone support-only contract starts at $50k p.a.
  2. An Open-Source solution that will be installed and supported by their internal IT department, relying on community (forum) support for any escalations. 
  3. An in-house created solution built by a student as part of her final year project and supported by the developer, as well as the internal IT department. 

Currently, all the resource planning and scheduling are done via spreadsheets and that solution is becoming unwieldy.

Your team has been retained as IT Risk Consultants with a brief to:

  1. Create a comprehensive risk assessment and report, recommending which of the three solutions the company should select. You are expected to justify your selection with supporting evidence.
  2. Create a Disaster Recovery solution for whichever option you recommend. The company has asked for a Recovery Point Objective of 15 minutes and a Recovery Time Objective of 4 hours. However, their production facility only runs 8 am to 6 pm so the company has asked for your professional recommendations as risk consultants.

Part 1: A Status Report Document

For this first assessment, your team is expected to propose an overview of your assumptions and scope of requirements for the organisation in the form of a status report. Note that the associated grading criteria are highlighted in the requirements below, to be reviewed alongside the criteria grid (Module Resources).

Your project status report should detail your assumptions around the Company's financial status and stability, risk appetite, IT capabilities, and company structure based on research of similar companies. This should come from a literature review of existing organisations of a similar size using similar technologies (such as using excel spreadsheets for resource planning and management, being a small manufacturing company, and similar criteria). (Knowledge and Understanding weighted at 25%)
  2. Based on your research, what business risks would you identify that the company may need to overcome. (Criticality weighted at 25%)
  3. 3. Enumerate the technical and business risks and assumptions you have made for all three solutions. (Application of Knowledge and Understanding weighted at 25%)

Initial Post
Post by Jurbe Jubet

Spears & Barki (2010) wrote an article on User Participation in Information Systems Security Risk Management(ISSRM) . The Article highlight the importance of involving Users in Security Risk Management.

In the study, they used a multi-method approach with both Qualitative and Quantitative assessment. The qualitative approach relied on interviewing users identified by an informant, as participating in ISSRM activities and controls in their respective Organisations. The Qualitative assessment was then tested by a Quantitative method. The reason being that combining qualitative and quantitative methods gave a better quality of results based on the ability to cross validate the accuracy of the qualitative assessment outcomes with quantitative methods(Spears & Barki, 2010).

The writers observed that involving Users in security risk management created an avenue for users to provide their expertise on how information is used in there day to day business activities. This helps management to understand how information is being used and where vulnerabilities lie, while also creating awareness to the users on the organization's risk tolerance, policies, and procedures. Also highlighted is that, the more users are engaged in information system development, the more they perceive it as personal and important. This creates better relationships with information security professionals and a better understanding of business needs(Spears & Barki, 2010).

Based on this report, it can be concluded that without effective user participation in ISSRM, there will be poor quality information (due to poor quality user feedback) used in risk assessment(Spears, 2006), thus impacting negatively on ISSRM.

I will use Quantitative approach and also rely on Historical data to create an initial assessment, and to develop matching probabilities(Sutton, 2021). Incorporating historical data from similar research to test a recent task provides more data that will be used to assess risk and also gives a guide on the expected outcome for more accurate predictions.

Post by Agne Angelides

Thank you Ags for this insight. I agree that assumptions based on historical data may introduce significant deviations that may impact on the organisations risk assessment. The limitation of this approach has been identified in this case, as due to lack of User access in risk assessment. However, an approach can be used to improve the quality of the assumptions and get a better risk Assessment outcome

(Khorsandi and Aven, 2017) suggest a framework which consist of 7 steps approach for evaluating the assumption deviation risk in Quality Risk Assessment (QRA), namely:

  1. Identifying the safety objectives for which the QRA aims to address/support.
  2. Identifying critical assumptions for which the results of the QRA are dependent upon, and parts of the QRA they affect.
  3. Identifying scenarios in which deviations in the assumptions identi- fied can lead to failures in meeting the established objectives.
  4. Assessing the likelihood of the assumption deviations leading to the unacceptable scenarios.
  5. Evaluating the strength of knowledge supporting the assessments.
  6. Evaluating the overall risk.
  7. Assessing assumption deviation risk with multiple assumptions (Khorsandi and Aven, 2017).

The Approach aims to test the level of assumption deviation by way of identifying which assumptions are high risk or Low risk, while providing the decision makers with better options in selecting the preferred assumptions. It also gives decision makers more insight on which of the assumptions will need more information gathering, or more control requirements(Khorsandi and Aven, 2017). This way, I belive that deviations in assumptions based on historical data, will be better managed
